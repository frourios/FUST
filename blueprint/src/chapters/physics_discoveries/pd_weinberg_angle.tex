\section{\texorpdfstring{$F_\zeta$}{Fζ} Channel Decomposition}
\label{sec:weinberg_channel}

The unified $D_\zeta$ operator output decomposes into two orthogonal channels
via the $\mathbb{Z}/6\mathbb{Z}$ Fourier structure:
\[
D_\zeta[f](z) = \frac{\mathrm{AFNum}(\Phi_A f)(z) + \mathrm{SymNum}(\Phi_S f)(z)}{z}
\]
where $\Phi_A$ (antisymmetric, rank 1) carries $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ and
$\Phi_S$ (symmetric, rank 3) carries $\mathrm{SU}(3)$.

\label{sec:weinberg_normSq}

\label{thm:weinberg_normSq}
\lean{FUST.WeinbergAngle.normSq_weight_decomposition}

\section{Weinberg Angle Formula}
\label{sec:weinberg_formula}

\begin{theorem}[Weinberg Angle from Channel Weights]
\label{thm:weinberg_formula}
\lean{FUST.WeinbergAngle.weinberg_angle_formula}
\leanok
\[
\sin^2\theta_W = \frac{\text{AF weight}}{\text{SY weight} + \text{AF weight}}
= \frac{1}{3 + 1} = \frac{1}{4} = 0.25

after renormalization group running.

\section{Connection to Gauge Boson Masses}
\label{sec:weinberg_gauge_mass}

The Weinberg angle directly determines the W/Z mass ratio:
\[
\frac{m_W}{m_Z} = \cos\theta_W = \sqrt{\frac{3}{4}} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866.
\]

Combined with $m_W/m_e = \varphi^{25} \times 15/16$ (Chapter~\ref{chap:pd_mass_ratios}):
\[
\frac{m_Z}{m_e} = \frac{m_W}{m_e} \times \frac{1}{\cos\theta_W}
= \varphi^{25} \times \frac{15}{16} \times \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}.

The Z boson mass is fully determined by $\varphi$ and $F_\zeta$ channel weights,
with no free parameters.

a formal sum of two ScaleQ with different FDim.
The $\mathbb{R}$-evaluation recovers the physical $m_Z^2 = m_W^2 \times 4/3$.
