\lean{FUST.NavierStokes.PlanckCutoff.ClayNSSolution}

\item[(vii)] \textbf{Dissipation active}: $t \geq 0,\; E_{\mathrm{high}}(t) > 0 \Rightarrow D_{\mathrm{high}}(t) > 0$
\item[(viii)] \textbf{$\ker(F_\zeta)$ invariant}: $f \in \ker(F_\zeta) \Rightarrow \mathrm{timeEvolution}(f) \in \ker(F_\zeta)$
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

Condition (iii) is the key: the solution is \emph{finite-dimensional}, with all modes above $N_{\max}$ identically zero. This is justified by $D_\zeta$'s Planck-scale thermal cutoff.
