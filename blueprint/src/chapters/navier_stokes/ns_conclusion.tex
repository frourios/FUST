\chapter{Relation to Standard NS and Conclusion}
\label{chap:ns_conclusion}

\section{On Equivalence}
\label{sec:ns_equivalence}

FUST is not ``equivalent'' to standard NS. This is intentional:

\begin{itemize}
\item Standard NS: Equation on continuous space $\mathbb{R}^3$
\item FUST-NS: Equation on discrete scales $\varphi^n$
\end{itemize}

\section{Why It Is Solvable in FUST}
\label{sec:ns_why_solvable}

The reason blowup can occur in standard NS is that the energy cascade to infinitesimal scales cannot be controlled. In FUST:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Discrete scales}: Continuous cascade to infinitesimals does not exist
\item \textbf{Algebraic structure}: Properties of the golden ratio guarantee dissipation
\item \textbf{Structural lower bound}: Dissipation at high modes is necessarily positive
\end{enumerate}

\section{Physical Interpretation}
\label{sec:ns_physical_interp}

Taking the continuous limit $\varphi^n \to k$ (continuous wavenumber) loses FUST's discrete structure. However:

\begin{itemize}
\item Physically, the continuous limit is an idealization
\item Below the Planck scale, continuous description loses meaning
\item FUST's discrete structure is physically natural
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:ns_final_conclusion}

Global existence for the Navier-Stokes type equation has been rigorously proven within the FUST framework. The core of the proof is:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Lower bound on dissipation}: $C_n \geq \frac{1}{3}\varphi^{3n}$ for $n \geq 4$
\item \textbf{Energy decay}: High mode energy necessarily has positive dissipation
\item \textbf{Kernel invariance}: Smooth states are preserved under time evolution
\end{enumerate}

These structurally forbid finite-time blowup and guarantee global existence.
