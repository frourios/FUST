\section{Overview: Gauge Freedom from Factorization}
\label{sec:gauge_overview}

The gauge group arises from the \textbf{input side}: the ambiguity in factoring a state function $f = f_1 \cdot f_2 \cdots f_k$. The derivation defect
\[
\delta(f,g) := F_\zeta(fg) - f \cdot F_\zeta(g) - F_\zeta(f) \cdot g
\]
measures how $F_\zeta$ fails to be a derivation. The key gauge invariance is:

\begin{theorem}[Factorization Gauge Invariance]
\label{thm:factorization_gauge}
\lean{FUST.FζOperator.derivDefect_const_gauge}
\leanok
For $c \in \mathbb{C}^\times$, $\delta(cf, c^{-1}g) = \delta(f,g)$.
\end{theorem}

This means the physics encoded in $\delta$ is invariant under $(f,g) \mapsto (cf, c^{-1}g)$, giving a $\mathrm{U}(1)$ scalar gauge. For multi-mode superpositions with $k$ modes, the mode coefficients can be mixed by $\mathrm{GL}(k)$ while preserving the product; unitarity and scalar separation then give $\mathrm{SU}(k)$. The maximum $k$ is bounded by the rank of the mode vector space.

The three channels of $\mathbb{Z}[\varphi, \zeta_6]$, classified by their Galois eigenvalues under $\sigma$ and $\tau$, uniquely determine the gauge group representations as $(3, 2, 1)$.

\section{SU(3) Uniqueness: SY Channel}
\label{sec:SU3_derivation}

For each active mode $s$, the $\Phi_S$ sub-operator eigenvalues give a \textbf{mode vector}:
\[
v(s) = (\sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_5}(s),\; \sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_3}(s),\; \sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_2}(s)) \in \mathbb{R}^3
\]
where $\sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_5}(s) = \varphi^{2s} + \varphi^s - 4 + \psi^s + \psi^{2s}$, $\sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_3}(s) = \varphi^s - 2 + \psi^s$, $\sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_2}(s) = \varphi^s - \psi^s$.

The $3 \times 3$ matrix
\[
M_{\mathrm{SY}} = \begin{pmatrix} \sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_5}(1) & \sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_5}(5) & \sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_5}(7) \\
\sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_3}(1) & \sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_3}(5) & \sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_3}(7) \\
\sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_2}(1) & \sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_2}(5) & \sigma_{\mathrm{Diff}_2}(7)
\end{pmatrix}
\]
has $\det M_{\mathrm{SY}} \neq 0$, so its rows are $\mathbb{R}$-linearly independent and the SY mode space has rank 3. Since $\dim \mathbb{R}^3 = 3$, any 4th mode vector is a linear combination of the first 3. Norm-preserving gauge transformations on 3 independent modes act via $\mathrm{U}(3)$. The scalar matrix $c \cdot I_3 \in \mathrm{U}(3)$ for $|c| = 1$, and $\det(c \cdot I_3) = c^3$. The Mathlib characterization $\mathrm{SU}(3) = \{M \in \mathrm{U}(3) \mid \det M = 1\}$ then gives:

\begin{theorem}[SU(3) Gauge Uniqueness]
\label{thm:SU3_gauge_uniqueness}
\lean{FUST.SU3_gauge_uniqueness}
\leanok
The SY channel gauge group is uniquely $\mathrm{SU}(3) = \mathrm{specialUnitaryGroup}\;(\mathrm{Fin}\;3)\;\mathbb{C}$, established by:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Mode vectors are linearly independent (rank 3)
\item Scalar unitary $c \cdot I_3 \in \mathrm{U}(3)$ (center)
\item $\det(c \cdot I_3) = c^3$ (scalar gauge has nontrivial determinant)
\item $\overline{\phantom{z}} \neq \mathrm{id}$ on $\mathbb{C}$ (complex conjugation is nontrivial, excludes $\mathrm{O}(3)$)
\item Scalar gauge is already accounted for by $\delta(cf, c^{-1}g) = \delta(f,g)$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

The logic: rank 3 $\to$ $\mathrm{U}(3)$, scalar $\mathrm{U}(1)$ center already separated by derivation defect gauge, remaining mode-mixing has $\det = 1$, giving $\mathrm{SU}(3)$. Since $F_\zeta$ acts on $\mathbb{C}$-valued functions where complex conjugation is nontrivial ($\overline{i} = -i \neq i$), the gauge group is unitary, not orthogonal: $\mathrm{O}(3)$ is excluded.

\section{SU(2) Uniqueness: AF Channel}
\label{sec:SU2_derivation}

The Galois automorphism $\tau: \zeta_6 \mapsto 1 - \zeta_6$ fixes $\varphi$ and satisfies $\tau^2 = \mathrm{id}$, so its eigenvalues are $\pm 1$. The AF coefficient $\mathrm{AF\_coeff\_gei} = \langle -2, 0, 4, 0 \rangle$ has eigenvalue $-1$: $\tau(\mathrm{AF\_coeff}) = -\mathrm{AF\_coeff}$. Furthermore $\mathrm{AF\_coeff}^2 = -12 < 0$ in $\mathbb{Z}[\varphi, \zeta_6]$, the algebraic $J^2 < 0$ condition characterizing quaternionic structure. A 1D-over-$\mathbb{C}$ space with such an anti-involution extends to a 2D-over-$\mathbb{C}$ quaternionic representation.

\begin{theorem}[SU(2) Gauge Uniqueness]
\label{thm:SU2_gauge_uniqueness}
\lean{FUST.SU2_gauge_uniqueness}
\leanok
The AF channel gauge group is uniquely $\mathrm{SU}(2) = \mathrm{specialUnitaryGroup}\;(\mathrm{Fin}\;2)\;\mathbb{C}$, established by:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\tau(\mathrm{AF\_coeff}) = -\mathrm{AF\_coeff}$ (quaternionic indicator, eigenvalue $-1$)
\item $\tau^2 = \mathrm{id}$ (involution) and $\tau$ preserves multiplication (ring automorphism)
\item $\mathrm{AF\_coeff}^2 = -12$ (quaternionic $J^2 < 0$ condition)
\item $\mathrm{Im}(\mathrm{AF\_coeff}) \neq 0$ (complex structure essential, excludes $\mathrm{SO}(3)$)
\item Scalar unitary $c \cdot I_2 \in \mathrm{U}(2)$, $\det(c \cdot I_2) = c^2$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

The logic: $\tau$-anti-invariance $\to$ quaternionic type $\to$ $\mathrm{Sp}(1) \cong \mathrm{SU}(2)$. The 1D-over-$\mathbb{C}$ AF space with quaternionic doubling gives $\mathbb{C}^2 = \mathbb{H}^1$. Norm preservation on $\mathbb{C}^2 \to \mathrm{U}(2)$, scalar separation $\to \mathrm{SU}(2)$. The key exclusion of $\mathrm{SO}(3)$: since $\mathrm{AF\_coeff} = 2i\sqrt{3} \notin \mathbb{R}$, the AF channel requires complex structure. Over $\mathbb{R}$, $J^2 \geq 0$ always, so quaternionic structure ($J^2 < 0$) is impossible---this forces $\mathrm{SU}(2)$, not $\mathrm{SO}(3) \cong \mathrm{SU}(2)/\{\pm I\}$.

\section{U(1) Uniqueness: Trivial Channel}
\label{sec:U1_derivation}

The trivial channel is the Galois-fixed subspace of $\mathbb{Z}[\varphi, \zeta_6]$.

\begin{theorem}[Galois-Fixed Subspace $= \mathbb{Z}$]
\label{thm:galois_fixed}
\lean{FUST.FrourioAlgebra.GoldenEisensteinInt.galois_fixed_iff}
\leanok
For $x = \langle a, b, c, d \rangle \in \mathbb{Z}[\varphi, \zeta_6]$:
\[
\sigma(x) = x \;\wedge\; \tau(x) = x \iff b = 0 \;\wedge\; c = 0 \;\wedge\; d = 0
\]
So the Galois-fixed subspace is $\{(a, 0, 0, 0)\} \cong \mathbb{Z}$, which is 1-dimensional.
\end{theorem}

Since $\mathrm{SU}(1)$ consists of $1 \times 1$ unitary matrices with $\det M = M_{00} = 1$, it is trivial: $\mathrm{SU}(1) = \{I\}$.

\begin{theorem}[U(1) Gauge Uniqueness]
\label{thm:U1_gauge_uniqueness}
\lean{FUST.U1_gauge_uniqueness}
\leanok
The trivial channel gauge group is uniquely $\mathrm{U}(1) = \mathrm{unitaryGroup}\;(\mathrm{Fin}\;1)\;\mathbb{C}$, established by:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Galois-fixed subspace $= \mathbb{Z}$ (1-dimensional)
\item Scalar gauge invariance: $\delta(cf, c^{-1}g) = \delta(f,g)$
\item Scalar unitary $c \cdot I_1 \in \mathrm{U}(1)$ for $|c| = 1$
\item $\det(c \cdot I_1) = c^1$ (linear, not higher power)
\item $\mathrm{SU}(1) = \{I\}$ is trivial: no $\mathrm{SU}$ reduction
\item Galois-fixed units are $\pm 1$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

The logic: Galois-fixed $\to$ dim 1 $\to$ $\mathrm{U}(1)$. Unlike $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ and $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ where the scalar $\mathrm{U}(1)$ center is factored out, on the 1D trivial channel $\mathrm{SU}(1) = \{I\}$ is trivial, so the full gauge group is $\mathrm{U}(1)$ itself.

\section{Summary}
\label{sec:gauge_summary}

The gauge group $\mathrm{SU}(3) \times \mathrm{SU}(2) \times \mathrm{U}(1)$ is uniquely determined by the $F_\zeta$ factorization structure:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{SU(3)}: SY mode space has rank 3 ($\det M_{\mathrm{SY}} \neq 0$), norm preservation $\to$ $\mathrm{U}(3)$, scalar $\mathrm{U}(1)$ center separation, $\det = 1$ $\to$ $\mathrm{SU}(3)$
\item \textbf{SU(2)}: AF coefficient has $\tau$-eigenvalue $-1$ (quaternionic type), $\mathrm{AF\_coeff}^2 = -12 < 0$ ($J^2$ condition), quaternionic doubling $\mathbb{C}^1 \to \mathbb{C}^2 = \mathbb{H}^1$, $\mathrm{Sp}(1) \cong \mathrm{SU}(2)$
\item \textbf{U(1)}: Galois-fixed subspace is 1-dimensional ($b = c = d = 0$), $\mathrm{SU}(1) = \{I\}$ trivial, full scalar gauge $= \mathrm{U}(1)$
\item \textbf{Channel independence}: $\zeta_6 \neq \varphi^n$ ($\mathrm{Im}(\zeta_6) \neq 0$ while $\varphi^n \in \mathbb{R}$)
\end{itemize}
