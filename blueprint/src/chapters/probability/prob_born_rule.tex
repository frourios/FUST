\chapter{Born Rule}

\section{Action and Expectation}

The FUST action:
\[
\mathcal{A}[f] = \int |D_6[f](x)|^2 \frac{dx}{x}
\]
equals the probability expectation:
\[
\mathcal{A}[f] = \mathbb{E}_{P_f}[|D_6[f]|^2]
\]

This shows Born rule is NOT a new axiom but a structural consequence
of the $D_6$ action.

\section{Discrete Version}

\begin{definition}[Discrete Expectation]
\label{def:discrete_expectation}
\lean{FUST.Probability.discreteExpectation}
\leanok
\[
\mathbb{E}_N[|D_6[f]|^2] := \frac{1}{2N+1} \sum_{k=-N}^{N} A_f(k)^2
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Born Rule (Discrete)]
\label{thm:born_rule}
\lean{FUST.Probability.born_rule_discrete}
\leanok
\[
\mathcal{A}_N[f] = (2N + 1) \cdot \log\varphi \cdot \mathbb{E}_N[|D_6[f]|^2]
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By definition:
\begin{align*}
\mathcal{A}_N[f] &= \sum_{k=-N}^{N} A_f(k)^2 \cdot \log\varphi \\
&= (2N+1) \cdot \log\varphi \cdot \frac{1}{2N+1} \sum_{k=-N}^{N} A_f(k)^2 \\
&= (2N+1) \cdot \log\varphi \cdot \mathbb{E}_N[|D_6[f]|^2]
\end{align*}
\end{proof}

\section{Physical Interpretation}

The Born rule $P \propto |\psi|^2$ in quantum mechanics is usually
postulated as a fundamental axiom. In FUST:

\begin{enumerate}
\item $|D_6[f]|^2$ naturally appears in the action functional
\item The $\varphi$-scale iteration generates a ``sample space''
\item Frequency counts give empirical probabilities
\item The action equals the expectation of $|D_6[f]|^2$
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Conclusion}: Born rule emerges from the $D_6$ structure
without being postulated.
